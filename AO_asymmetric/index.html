Abstract

This work introduces the first closed-loop adaptive optics (AO) system capable of optically correcting aberrations in real-time without a guidestar or a wavefront sensor[cite: 31]. Inspired by recent advances in phase retrieval, we introduce a guidestar-free AO framework built around asymmetric apertures and machine learning[cite: 34]. Our approach combines an asymmetric aperture, neural networks for PSF and phase estimation, and a spatial light modulator for optical correction[cite: 35]. Experimental results show our method outperforms state-of-the-art methods using an order of magnitude fewer measurements and three orders of magnitude less computation[cite: 39].

Breaking Ambiguity with Asymmetry

For symmetric apertures, the phase $\phi(x,y)$ and its conjugate-flipped counterpart $\phi(-x,-y)$ produce identical PSFs, leading to ambiguity[cite: 102]. By using an asymmetric triangular aperture, we yield distinct PSFs for different wavefronts, enabling unique phase retrieval[cite: 103, 104].

Closed-Loop AO Pipeline

  • PSF U-Net: Estimates the PSF from captured sensor images broken into patches[cite: 167].
  • Phase U-Net: Reconstructs the phase aberration from the estimated PSF[cite: 192].
  • Optical Correction: The conjugate phase $(-\hat{\phi})$ is added to the SLM to correct aberrations iteratively[cite: 153].

Experimental Results

We validated our framework using real-world obscurants, including nail polish, onion skin, and diffusers[cite: 382]. Our method achieves high-quality image restoration with only four measurements[cite: 240].
